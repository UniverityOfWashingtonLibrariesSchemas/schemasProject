Definition

A name for the resource.
Input instructions

A brief descriptive phrase that includes these elements, generally in this order: who, what, where, when.

The Title is a concise sentence which will be used to label the thumbnails for the compound objects in CONTENTdm; it will also be the first line of the descriptive information. The Title may be a caption assigned by a photographer/creator/contributor or the collector, or it may be created by Special Collections staff from the given caption and from additional sources. In the absence of any caption, the Title will be provided by Special Collections staff.

If the caption provided by a photographer/creator/contributor is adequate, place it in the Title field. Then, the Notes field should include a phrase like this: "Title taken from photograph" or "Title taken from text attached to resource."

If the caption provided by a photographer/creator/contributor is inadequate, a created title should be placed in the Title field; the Notes field should then read something like: 'Caption on image: "Image title" ' or 'Title provided by University of Washington Libraries staff.'

If there is a series caption, it should be added in the Notes field; the note should begin "Series Title: "; the note should be in sequence after the notes describing the title (for example, the 'Caption on image: "Image title" ' note). There should also be a separate notation in the Notes field in the form: Part of Hegg series entitled: "Series title."

Input examples
