1 Title title yes no yes no

A name given to the resource.

A brief descriptive phrase that includes these elements, generally in this order: who, what, where, when.

The Title is a concise sentence which will be used to label the thumbnails in CONTENTdm; it will also be the first line of the descriptive information. The Title may be the caption assigned by the photographer or the collector, or it may be created by Special Collections staff from the given caption and from additional sources. In the absence of any caption, the Title will be provided by Special Collections staff.

If the caption provided by the photographer is adequate, place it in the Title field. Then, the Notes field should include this phrase: Title taken from photograph.

If the caption provided by the photographer is inadequate, a created title should be placed in the Title field; the Notes field should then read: Caption on image: "Image title."

If there is a series caption, it should be added, in the Notes field, in sequence after the title on the image: Caption on image: "Image title"; "Series title." There should also be a separate notation in the Notes field in the form: Part of Hegg series entitled: "Series title."

Alger house, interior view of living room Seattle, Washington, 1955

Woman mushing wood, Chilkoot Trail, ca. 1898

3 Date Edtf date no no yes no

Date the original resource was created.

Express all dates, including date ranges, using Library of Congress' Extended Date Time Format.

Most recent version of EDTF available at https://www.loc.gov/standards/datetime/pre-submission.html.

If the Date field = "1940," Date EDTF = "1940"

If the Date field = "ca. 1910," Date EDTF = "1905/1915"

If the Date field = "Approximately 1930," Date EDTF = "1930~"

If Date field = "Possibly 1901," Date EDTF = "1901?"

If Date field = "May 9, 2001,: Date EDTF = "2001-05-09"

If Date field = "January 2008," Date EDTF = "2008-01"

EDTF is still a standard in progress. It has not yet been accpted as a full recommendation.

This document needs a reference to the spec.

For the xsd, datatype is xs:string; the datatype is more accurately http://id.loc.gov/datatypes/edtf, which is not a valid QName and is inavlid according to schema-4-properties-0.1, but this does not seem to be machine-actionable; more exploration needed.

4 Date created no no yes no

Date of creation of the resource.

If the resource is a photograph, the date the original photograph was taken.

In cases where a painting, engraving, or architectural drawing was photographed, the date the artwork was created.

If the date when the image/resource in hand was produced is known, and is different from that of the original image, so note in the Notes field. The date should be a specific year. If the date is unknown, an attempt should be made to assign an approximate date, using the form "ca." (circa).

In CONTENTdm, this field is used for display purposes and should be user-friendly. The Dates field is indexed, enabling searching (see that entry for details). Specific dates (e.g., September 12, 1933; June 1912) should be entered in this field, and should also be incorporated in the Title field.

5 Dates none yes yes yes no

Date of creation of the resource.

If the resource is a photograph, the date the original photograph was taken.

In cases where a painting, engraving, or architectural drawing was photographed, the date the artwork was created.

Property to enable date searching in CONTENTdm.

Reflects the Date field. If the date is a single year, it is the same in both the Date and the Dates fields. If the date in the Date field is a "circa" date (e.g. ca. 1895), the Dates field contains the expanded version so that searching will find dates covered by the concept of a "circa" date. Five years on either side of the "ca." date is the preferred form.

If the Date field reads "ca. 1910" enter "1905, 1906, 1907, 1908, 1909, 1910, 1911, 1912, 1913, 1914, 1915"

1905 1906 1907 1908 1909 1910 1911 1912 1913 1914 1915
6 Notes description yes no no no

An account of the resource.

Notes include any information of importance that is not represented elsewhere.

Each separate "note" should be a paragraph. Additional notes can be added, separated from the previous note using a blank line.

Photographs taken by Asahel Curtis during his trip to the Klondike in 1897-1899.

Caption on image: Curtis 319.

Handwritten on verso: Mrs. McLain with dog team.

PH Coll 519.319

"Notes" is distingushed from "Historical Notes" mainly because it descibes the image/resource only. Values in this field also may be considered more "indexable" insofar as they precisely descibe the image/resource. "HistoricalNotes" are more like "writing" and create a braod historical context.
